Heyo,

                    I've been meaning to say this for a while now but never really found the way to put it into
                    words. I think, on some level, I still don't really know, but I want to try. And that is, I don't
                    think you know how much you actually inspire me.
                    Back in high school, my college counselor told me that I was really interesting to talk to in a way
                    that a lot of other typical high-GPA high-SAT kids weren't. Now, I still don't know why she said
                    that, because
                    I still feel quite normal. But I know you say that I don't have much to talk about, and that what I
                    talk about isn't interesting, and that makes me think about that comment all those years ago.
                    You're right about that, you know. I feel like I have become somewhat less interesting since high
                    school, once there wasn't anybody else to push me towards new things, no English class to force me
                    to read, no history to get me to study, and everything has kind of blended into whatever my brain
                    chooses to indulge in -- which is, oftentimes, pure slop.
                    But talking to you, watching you, makes me want to be more than that. Your brilliant brain -- yes,
                    it is brilliant, and you can never convince me otherwise -- sees things differently than mine
                    sometimes, and sees things MORE, oftentimes. Where I see three colors, you see seven; where I see
                    one path forward, you show me two more.
                    And watching you, seeing you, hearing you, and all your expectations and wants -- they inspire me to
                    be more than I am. To pick up that pen, to pick up that book, to give me a little more reason and
                    motivation to do anytihng. I've talked to a lot of people, people who are working, people who
                    aren't, and many of them I find are settled. They're content -- exactly that. Not too happy with
                    where they are but happy enough to not want to change it. And they do things, yes, but the same
                    things, every day, and for good reason, because they are content. I probably would've fallen in the
                    same category, if not for you. If not for your very existance driving me to be MORE, to be BETTER.
                    And even though better might be coming slowly, its coming. I promise.
                    Thank you for being you. Thank you for everything. I < 3 you, forever & infinity.

                    -- Momo
